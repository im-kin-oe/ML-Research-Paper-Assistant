Title: SafeAdapt : Provably Safe Policy Updates in Deep Reinforcement Learning

**TL;DR:** This paper proposes a novel approach to safe policy updates in continual reinforcement learning, introducing the "Rashomon set" - a certified region in policy parameter space that guarantees safety on previously encountered tasks. The method, called SafeAdapt, enables provably safe policy updates without requiring full knowledge of transition dynamics or runtime action filtering.

**Problem:** The problem addressed in this paper is how to update a reinforcement learning policy while preserving its safety properties on previously encountered tasks, especially in non-stationary environments or when the performance goals change.

**Method:** The authors propose a novel approach called SafeAdapt, which leverages the Local Invariant Domain (LID) framework to construct a certified region in policy parameter space, known as the Rashomon set. This set is computed using Interval Bound Propagation (IBP) and a differentiable safety surrogate, ensuring that every policy inside the set satisfies the source-task safety specification under greedy deployment. The authors then perform downstream adaptation with constraints by combining PPO updates with projected gradient descent, guaranteeing that parameter updates remain inside the certified safe region throughout training.

**Results:** The authors evaluate their method across grid-world navigation environments (Poisoned Apple and Frozen Lake) and demonstrate that their approach preserves source-task safety after adaptation while achieving competitive downstream performance. They also show that continual learning methods without formal guarantees (e.g., EWC) can lead to catastrophic forgetting of safe behavior in the source task, while their method is able to prevent this.

**Why it matters:** This research is important for anyone learning machine learning because it addresses a critical challenge in reinforcement learning: ensuring safety when updating policies in non-stationary environments. The proposed approach provides a formal guarantee of safety, which is essential for deploying RL agents in safety-critical domains, such as autonomous vehicles, medical treatment planning, and industrial process control.

**3 things to remember:**
- The Rashomon set is a certified region in policy parameter space that guarantees safety on previously encountered tasks.
- SafeAdapt is a novel approach that leverages the LID framework and IBP to compute the Rashomon set and perform downstream adaptation with constraints.
- The method provides a formal guarantee of safety, which is essential for deploying RL agents in safety-critical domains, and prevents catastrophic forgetting of safe behavior in the source task.
