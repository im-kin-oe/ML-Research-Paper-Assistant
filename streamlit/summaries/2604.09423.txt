Title: Offline Local Search for Online Stochastic Bandits

**TL;DR:** This paper proposes a framework for converting offline local search algorithms into online stochastic combinatorial bandit algorithms, achieving poly-logarithmic regret guarantees. The framework is applied to various problems, including scheduling, matroid optimization, and clustering.

**Problem:** The paper addresses the problem of online combinatorial optimization with bandit feedback, where the goal is to minimize regret, defined as the loss compared to the optimal fixed action in hindsight.

**Method:** The authors propose a framework for converting offline local search algorithms into online stochastic combinatorial bandit algorithms. The framework requires the local search neighborhood to admit (β, γ)-improving moves, which means that the neighborhood of a solution contains a β-factor improvement over the current solution. The algorithm attempts to mimic the offline local search algorithm while handling the complications of bandit feedback.

**Results:** The authors achieve γ-regret guarantees that scale as O(log^3 T), which is a substantially better dependence on T than prior frameworks. They apply their framework to various problems, including scheduling, matroid optimization, and clustering, and obtain regret bounds that are poly-logarithmic in T.

**Why it matters:** This paper is important because it provides a general framework for converting offline algorithms into online bandit algorithms, which can be applied to a wide range of problems. The poly-logarithmic regret guarantees are particularly significant, as they improve upon the typical O(T^ρ) regret bounds achieved by prior frameworks.

**3 things to remember:**
- The framework requires the local search neighborhood to admit (β, γ)-improving moves, which is a key property that enables the algorithm to achieve poly-logarithmic regret guarantees.
- The algorithm attempts to mimic the offline local search algorithm while handling the complications of bandit feedback, which involves estimating the cost of a solution by sampling and exploring the neighborhood of the current solution.
- The framework is applied to various problems, including scheduling, matroid optimization, and clustering, and obtains regret bounds that are poly-logarithmic in T, which is a significant improvement over prior frameworks.
