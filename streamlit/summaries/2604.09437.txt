Title: AdaCubic : An Adaptive Cubic Regularization Optimizer for Deep Learning

**TL;DR:** This paper proposes a novel regularization technique called AdaCubic, which adapts the weight of the cubic term in Newton's cubic regularized method to avoid saddle points in non-convex optimization problems. AdaCubic outperforms or competes with several widely used optimizers in various tasks.

**Problem:** The paper addresses the problem of avoiding saddle points in non-convex optimization problems, which can arise during the training of deep neural networks. Saddle points can slow down the convergence of optimization algorithms and lead to suboptimal solutions.

**Method:** The authors propose AdaCubic, a novel regularization technique that adapts the weight of the cubic term in Newton's cubic regularized method. AdaCubic uses an auxiliary optimization problem with cubic constraints to dynamically adjust the weight of the cubic term. The algorithm leverages Hutchinson's method to approximate the Hessian matrix, reducing computational cost.

**Results:** The authors demonstrate that AdaCubic inherits the local convergence guarantees of the cubically regularized Newton method. Experimental results show that AdaCubic outperforms or competes with several widely used optimizers, including SGD, Adam, and AdaHessian, in various tasks such as computer vision, natural language processing, and signal processing.

**Why it matters:** AdaCubic is an attractive optimizer for deep learning applications because it does not require hyperparameter fine-tuning, making it a highly attractive option for researchers and practitioners. Additionally, AdaCubic is the first optimizer to leverage cubic regularization in scalable deep learning applications.

**3 things to remember:**
- AdaCubic adapts the weight of the cubic term in Newton's cubic regularized method to avoid saddle points in non-convex optimization problems.
- The algorithm uses an auxiliary optimization problem with cubic constraints to dynamically adjust the weight of the cubic term.
- AdaCubic outperforms or competes with several widely used optimizers in various tasks, making it a promising optimizer for deep learning applications.
